# Verification Protocol

BrandTrace is a source-based ownership database. Verification is the process that protects the platform from unsupported claims, outdated ownership records, and unclear corporate relationships.

## Core Rule

No ownership claim should be marked as verified unless it is supported by reliable documentation.

## Information Statuses

- **Verified Information:** Reviewed and supported by reliable sources.
- **Pending Review:** Submitted or imported information awaiting reviewer evaluation.
- **Community Submission:** User-provided information that has not been verified.
- **Opinion:** Subjective commentary that must not be presented as factual ownership data.

## Preferred Source Types

Examples of stronger sources include:

- Company annual reports.
- Securities filings.
- Official acquisition announcements.
- Corporate websites and ownership pages.
- Government business registries.
- Court or regulatory filings.
- Reputable journalism that cites primary sources.

## Source Requirements

Each verified record should include, whenever possible:

- Source title.
- Source publisher or authority.
- Source URL or reference identifier.
- Date published or accessed.
- Relevant excerpt or summary.
- Reviewer notes.
- Verification date.

## Review Workflow

1. A record is submitted or imported as pending review.
2. A reviewer evaluates source quality and relevance.
3. The reviewer confirms that the source supports the specific claim.
4. Conflicting evidence is documented rather than ignored.
5. The record is approved, rejected, returned for more evidence, or marked disputed.
6. Approved records become verified information.

## Corrections

Corrections should be handled carefully and transparently. A correction should include:

- The record being challenged.
- The proposed correction.
- Supporting sources.
- Reviewer decision.
- Change history.

## Neutrality

Verification confirms whether a claim is adequately supported. It does not endorse a political, social, or consumer action.
